My interests

I explore the intersection of design, sustainability, and technology. Seeing their overlaps is a exciting and enriching experience. My work fits under the umbrellas of UX design, human computer interaction, and sustainable development.

Defining Sustainability +

Sustainability is a holistic approach that tackles complex social, economic, and environmental challenges through interdisciplinary solutions. I believe the most effective sustainable outcomes are not the quickest or most buzz-worthy ideas but those rooted in systemic thinking for societal transformation. Additionally, I am interested in how we communicate and build sustainability activism, particularly through visual storytelling. My interest stems from my certified organic family farm and multiple environmental experiences.

Defining Technology +

Technology is often portrayed as the solution for solving complex societal problems, often overshadowing the importance of human-centered, community-driven approaches. Sustainable solutions come from balancing both technological and human factors for long-term impact. I am particularly interested in the relationship between content moderation and internet censorship, specifically within educational settings. My interest stems from my passion for social issues and conducting ethnographic research into youth digital rights.

Defining HCI Sustainable Design +

HCI Sustainable Design sits at the intersection of sustainability and technology, aiming for impactful design. This approach emphasizes creating ethical digital platforms and systems that prioritize sustainability principles throughout its entire development. processes. HCI Sustainable Design strives to create technology that supports a sustainable future for both people and the planet.
