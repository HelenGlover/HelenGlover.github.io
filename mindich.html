Mindich Fellowship

An education situated in collective potential, rooted in design

Timeline: January 2024
Role: Mindich Fellow

Overview

The challenges of our time require that we imagine new systems and structures to support one another as we build new futures. In envisioning the future of education, I believe in a transformative education situated in collective potential and rooted in participatory design.

In January 2024, I worked as the Mindich Fellowship at LightHouse Holyoke, an entirely personalized and competency-based school for grades 6 - 12. My work focused on exploring the power of design activism as a catalyst for social change, with a focus on the art of storytelling. I was interested in exploring design in two ways: as a tool for students to create their career paths and as a practice for addressing societal challenges through creative problem-solving. The goal was to see how to best support students in learning about and applying design based on their educational context.

  • What does design mean to you? How do you design?
  • How do you engage with design in your life?
  • What do you believe in? How can you connect your personal values, identity, and motivations to potential career paths?
  • Explaining how what they are doing at the school is preparing them to engage with larger societal, cultural, and spiritual challenges or questions
  • How are the activities and learning experiences you are doing here preparing you to tackle issues you care about in the world?
  • How do you use/want to use storytelling to inspire others and promote change?
