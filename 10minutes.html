Defining Sustainability

Sustainability is a holistic approach that tackles complex social, economic, and environmental challenges through interdisciplinary solutions. I believe the most effective sustainable outcomes are not the quickest or most buzz-worthy ideas, but those rooted in systemic thinking for societal transformation. Additionally, I am interested in how we communicate and build sustainability activism, particularly through visual storytelling. My interest stems from my experience in environmental internships and interdisciplinary courses in landscape studies, design, and ecology.


Defining Technology

In this techno-solutionist society, technology is seen as the primary driver for solving complex problems and achieving progress, often overshadowing the importance of human-centered approaches and social considerations. I am particularly interested in the relationship between content moderation and internet censorship, specifically within educational settings and the digital landscape of play. My interest stems from my ethnographic research into youth digital rights.


HCI Sustainable Design

HCI Sustainable Design is the intersection of sustainability and technology through purposeful design. This approach focuses on building ethical digital platforms and systems that prioritize long-term environmental health and social well-being. By integrating principles of sustainability into the design and development process, HCI Sustainable Design aims to create technology that supports a sustainable future for both people and the planet.

Animating Questions

Guided by my main question, "How can we co-create a society that is ecologically sound, socially just, and economically viable?" in my career, I seek to understand and explore other animating questions:

  • How can we use sustainability as an integrative approach that addresses technological and human needs?
  • How can we apply sustainability's restoration, regeneration, and resilience elements within the world of technology?
